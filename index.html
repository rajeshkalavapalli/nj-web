Welcome to Navajeevan Organization

Genisis Of The Organisation

Despite Andhra Pradesh being considered one of the pioneering states in the country, the issue of increasing poverty remains prevalent. While the area is experiencing development, a significant majority of people continue to live in acute poverty. Unfortunately, the opportunities and programs intended for the poor have not effectively reached the real beneficiaries. In this context, Mr. K. Sahadevaiah and like-minded colleagues have committed themselves to the development of the impoverished. It was their personal dedication that led to the establishment of Navajeevan in 1996.

Navajeevan is a mass initiative dedicated to the integrated development of primitive tribal, scheduled caste, and tribal community people in the remote villages of Nellore, Kadapa, and Chittoor Districts, as well as coastal communities along the Bay of Bengal. These communities face significant challenges: They rely solely on daily fishing and agricultural labor for their livelihoods. They face food insecurity when there is no work available. Due to these difficulties, many community members are forced to migrate to other regions in search of sustainable livelihoods. This situation underscores the urgent need for sustainable development initiatives to uplift these communities and provide them with better opportunities for a dignified life.

In response to these severe challenges, a group of social development activists has resolved to address the issues faced by the people in this region by utilizing locally available resources. The aim is to create employment opportunities for deserving families in the villages. Initially, we focused on the effective development of women and children. We conducted Participatory Rural Appraisal (PRA) in all our adopted villages to understand the situation and problems of the local people. The people in this region face significant challenges such as illiteracy, lack of proper healthcare facilities, unemployment, migration, and inadequate water for irrigation. Given these issues, the community has prioritized the eradication of illiteracy. To transform this vision into reality, K. Sahadevaiah, the President, along with six like-minded individuals who form the governing body of Navajeevan, have decided to initiate integrated development projects. These projects aim to promote sustainable development among the aboriginal and other downtrodden communities in the region. K. Sahadevaiah himself has approached several organizations for support and guidance. Mr. K. Sahadevaiah is a renowned social activist in the area who has dedicated himself to raising awareness among these communities about their rights and responsibilities.

MAJOR OBJECTIVES OF THE ORGANISATION:

1.To foster the development of the women children disadvantaged marginalized groups in the society.

2.Empowering the poor by way of offering services to give minimum access to development through rights approach

3.Streamlining the holistic development of the poor and vulnerable in the society that is leading hand to mouth and precarious living conditions.

4.To motivate and education the masses as about their own development and to prepare them to tap all resources meant for them.
